CodePen and CodinGame

Oct 29th, 2015 2:13 pm

CodinGame

So I found CodinGame off of an advertisement on Facebook - its pretty nice. Its mostly just a bunch of various coding challenges centered around computer science principles coupled with gamification, but gamification does work. The feel of everything is right as well - the design is functional and easy to use, and the onboarding process is done very well. It guides you through using the browser-based IDE and the first of many challenges, effectively teaching you the basics of its interface.

On the other hand, trying to find friends and followers is a bit of a pain - there’s a lot to be said for iconography as a navigational choice, but not all of it is kind. Particularly if the iconography is new or different when compared to similar websites. For instance, the “Home” button doesn’t bring you to where you would expect - it brings you to what appears to be a page to sign up for a contest. In order to get to some sort of dashboard, you have to click on your profile, where you can find an overview of your activity on the site.

Of course, there is the question of what they want to drive traffic to (which is presumably the contests) but regardless, home should bring you to a section of the site where things are familiar and you feel like you are in control. This just brings you to a sign-up page for a very specific feature on the site.

CodePen

CodePen is a pretty amazing website - the UI is great, the design is both usable and beautiful, and the onboarding process was done quite well. It is essentially a public code repository, which is amazingly useful for pretty much anything, including showcasing your own work. I can see that I will learn quite a lot by using CodePen as a fun environment to test out new designs and concepts.
